# Day +6 Action Plan - Medical, Benefits, Lifestyle

Public-safe version of a private daily plan. Exact calendar date is intentionally omitted.

## Purpose

Move the case forward on three fronts:

- medical staging and treatment planning;
- financial, VA, work-leave, and insurance protection;
- treatment-readiness habits for nutrition, exercise, oral care, sleep, and documentation.

## Top Three Outcomes

If only three things get done:

- [ ] Contact the care team to confirm next-step staging, oncology referral path, tumor-board path, and oncology social work.
- [ ] Protect benefits/financial position by starting VA claim-date protection or contacting a VSO/accredited representative, and checking SSA disability eligibility basics.
- [ ] Maintain body-readiness baseline: protein, hydration, walking, oral care, symptom/weight tracking, no alcohol/nicotine.

## Medical Actions

Contact the care team and ask:

- [ ] Who owns the next step: ENT, oncology, radiation oncology, community care, or tumor board?
- [ ] Is PET/CT or other staging imaging ordered?
- [ ] If `M0` is being reported, what imaging or assessment is it based on?
- [ ] Is the case being reviewed by a head-and-neck tumor board?
- [ ] What is the primary-site workup plan?
- [ ] What is the exact working diagnosis today: HPV-mediated oropharyngeal SCC, SCC of unknown primary, or another head/neck primary?
- [ ] What are cT, cN, cM, AJCC 8 stage if oropharyngeal, node size/laterality, and extranodal-extension suspicion?
- [ ] Should PD-L1 CPS be ordered now?
- [ ] Should HPV DNA/RNA or genotype testing be ordered beyond p16?
- [ ] Is treatment expected to be surgery-based, definitive chemoradiation-based, or still undecided?
- [ ] Does perioperative pembrolizumab apply only if the exact surgery-based/resectable/PD-L1 CPS criteria fit?
- [ ] Should HPV ctDNA / TTMV-HPV DNA be drawn before treatment starts?
- [ ] Are `NCT06223568` or `NCT04900623` worth screening before treatment begins, without delaying curative care?
- [ ] Should dental clearance, speech/swallow, nutrition, audiology, or fertility preservation be started before treatment?
- [ ] Can oncology social work help with benefits, travel, lodging, work paperwork, financial hardship, and local assistance?

## VA / Benefits / Financial Actions

### VA Track

- [ ] Protect the date with VA Intent to File if service connection may be pursued.
- [ ] Contact a VSO or accredited representative.
- [ ] Explain the generalized exposure frame: former submarine service with reported AFFF/PFAS exposure; current metastatic SCC in a patient-right cervical lymph node; p16 positive; primary/stage pending.
- [ ] Ask whether AFFF/PFAS should be framed as direct service connection / TERA.
- [ ] Ask whether PACT Act presumptions apply based on private service locations or operations.
- [ ] Ask whether radiation, asbestos, solvents, shipyard, fuel/oil, or other occupational exposure records should be reviewed.
- [ ] Keep boat, hull, command, deployment, witness, and exact service details in the private claim file only.

### SSA Track

- [ ] Check `my Social Security` account access.
- [ ] Check SSDI insured status and estimated benefit privately.
- [ ] Begin Adult Disability Starter Kit / Adult Disability Report draft if work ability is impaired or likely to be impaired.
- [ ] Do not assume Compassionate Allowance unless final staging/resectability evidence supports it.
- [ ] Track work limitations, reduced hours, missed work, treatment side effects, and physician restrictions.

### Employer / Insurance Track

- [ ] Ask HR or benefits administrator about FMLA, ADA accommodations, short-term disability, long-term disability, PTO, health insurance continuation, COBRA, and life-insurance conversion/accelerated-benefit rules.
- [ ] Do not resign or permanently reduce work before understanding benefits and insurance consequences.
- [ ] Ask who receives physician work-restriction paperwork.

### Expense Tracking

- [ ] Start a private expense log for mileage, parking, tolls, lodging, copays, medications, dental costs, nutrition supplies, missed work, and other out-of-pocket costs.
- [ ] Keep receipts and claim numbers out of the public repo.

## Lifestyle / Diet / Exercise

Goal: maintain weight, muscle, and treatment readiness. Avoid crash dieting and unapproved supplement protocols.

### Nutrition

- [ ] Protein target: roughly 95-120 g/day if no clinician has advised protein restriction.
- [ ] Eat three protein anchors: breakfast, lunch, dinner.
- [ ] Add high-protein snacks if appetite is low.
- [ ] Aim for five or more plant servings across vegetables, fruit, beans/lentils, and whole grains.
- [ ] Hydrate consistently.
- [ ] Avoid processed meat and keep highly processed foods low.
- [ ] Zero alcohol.
- [ ] No tobacco/nicotine/vaping; ask the care team for cessation help if needed.

Simple options:

- Greek yogurt, eggs, oats, berries, nuts.
- Chicken, salmon, tofu, beans, greens, rice, olive oil.
- Protein shake, cottage cheese, tuna/egg salad, hummus.
- Soft fallback: smoothie, soup with blended beans/lentils, oatmeal, mashed potatoes with protein, soft fish.

### Exercise

- [ ] Walk 30-45 minutes easy, or split into shorter walks.
- [ ] Strength work only if energy is good and symptoms allow.
- [ ] Keep several reps in reserve; no max efforts.
- [ ] Stop for chest pain, fainting, severe shortness of breath, neurologic symptoms, rapidly worsening swelling, bleeding, or airway/swallowing danger.

### Oral Care

- [ ] Brush morning and night with gentle fluoride toothpaste.
- [ ] Floss gently.
- [ ] Avoid whitening, peroxide, charcoal, and harsh abrasive products.
- [ ] Ask dental/radiation oncology about prescription high-fluoride toothpaste or fluoride trays if head-and-neck radiation is possible.

### Sleep / Stress

- [ ] Fixed wake time.
- [ ] Morning outdoor light.
- [ ] Caffeine cutoff well before bedtime.
- [ ] Ten-minute downshift: breathing, prayer, meditation, journaling, or quiet walk.
- [ ] Protect enough time in bed.

## Documentation Actions

- [ ] Update private weight and symptom logs.
- [ ] Add new medical/financial facts to the private parent vault first.
- [ ] Keep raw portal text, screenshots, DICOM files, claim numbers, service identifiers, and exact private claim details out of this repository.
- [ ] If new results arrive, create a private note first; later convert only the public-safe summary.

- [ ] Start VA Intent to File or contact one VSO.
- [ ] Eat three protein feedings.
- [ ] Walk 10 minutes.
- [ ] Brush/floss.
- [ ] Log symptoms, weight, expenses, and next actions privately.

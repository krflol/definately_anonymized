# Glossary - Head And Neck Cancer Terms

This glossary is intentionally broad and public-safe.

## Diagnostic Terms

**Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC)**: A cancer arising from squamous cells, which line surfaces such as the mouth, throat, and skin.

**Metastatic**: Cancer found away from where it started. In a neck lymph node, it usually means the cancer spread from a primary site nearby. It does not automatically mean distant stage IV metastatic disease.

**Primary site**: The place the cancer started.

**Occult primary**: Cancer has been found in a lymph node, but the starting site has not yet been found.

**Cervical lymph node**: A lymph node in the neck.

**Conglomerate**: Multiple lymph nodes or masses that appear joined together.

**Pathology**: Lab examination of tissue.

**Core needle biopsy**: A biopsy that removes small cylinders of tissue with a needle.

**Flow cytometry**: Lab test often used to evaluate blood/lymphoid cancers. A normal or non-diagnostic result can make lymphoma less likely but does not replace pathology.

## Biomarkers

**p16**: A protein used as a surrogate marker for HPV-mediated oropharyngeal cancer when staining is strong and diffuse.

**p16 block staining**: A staining pattern that supports HPV-mediated disease in the right clinical context.

**HPV-mediated**: Cancer driven by human papillomavirus.

**HPV16**: A high-risk HPV type commonly involved in HPV-associated oropharyngeal cancer.

**PD-L1 CPS**: Combined Positive Score. A pathology score that may affect immunotherapy eligibility in some settings.

**CK5**: A squamous-marker stain that supports squamous-cell differentiation.

## Staging And Treatment-Planning Terms

**TNM stage**: Tumor-node-metastasis staging system. It describes primary tumor size/extent, lymph nodes, and distant spread.

**Regional disease**: Cancer spread within nearby lymph nodes or structures.

**Distant metastasis**: Cancer spread to distant organs or non-regional sites.

**Resectable**: Surgically removable with an acceptable plan for cancer control and function.

**Unresectable**: Not reasonably removable by surgery, often because of anatomy, extent, risk, or inability to get adequate margins.

**Inoperable**: Surgery is not appropriate, often because of patient health, anatomy, or risk.

**Curative intent**: Treatment is being planned with the goal of cure.

**Definitive chemoradiation**: Radiation plus chemotherapy used as the main curative treatment, without surgery as the primary removal method.

**Adjuvant therapy**: Treatment given after the main treatment to reduce recurrence risk.

**Neoadjuvant therapy**: Treatment given before the main treatment.

**Perioperative therapy**: Treatment given around surgery, usually before and after.

**Tumor board**: A multidisciplinary meeting where specialists discuss diagnosis and treatment planning.

## Treatment Terms

**Cisplatin**: A chemotherapy drug commonly used with radiation for some head-and-neck cancers.

**Cetuximab**: An EGFR-targeted drug used in selected head-and-neck cancer settings.

**Pembrolizumab**: A PD-1 immunotherapy drug.

**Nivolumab**: A PD-1 immunotherapy drug.

**Checkpoint inhibitor**: Immunotherapy that releases immune-system brakes so immune cells may attack cancer.

**Therapeutic HPV vaccine**: A treatment vaccine intended to help the immune system attack HPV-driven cancer cells.

**mRNA vaccine**: A vaccine platform that delivers instructions for cells to make a target antigen, helping train the immune system.

**TCR-T therapy**: A cell therapy where T cells are engineered to recognize a specific tumor target.

**CRISPR**: A gene-editing technology. For HPV-associated head-and-neck cancer, direct CRISPR treatment remains research-stage, not standard care.

**ctDNA**: Circulating tumor DNA found in blood.

**TTMV-HPV DNA**: Tumor-tissue-modified viral HPV DNA; a blood-based marker used in some HPV-associated cancer monitoring contexts.

**De-intensification**: Reducing treatment intensity to lower toxicity. In HPV-positive head-and-neck cancer, this should be carefully selected and often trial-based.

## Supportive Care Terms

**Dysphagia**: Trouble swallowing.

**Xerostomia**: Dry mouth.

**Mucositis**: Painful inflammation/sores in the mouth or throat, often from radiation or chemotherapy.

**Feeding tube**: A tube used to support nutrition when swallowing is unsafe or inadequate.

**Audiology**: Hearing evaluation. Important because cisplatin can affect hearing.

**Speech/swallow therapy**: Therapy to preserve or recover speaking and swallowing function.

**Dental clearance**: Dental evaluation before head-and-neck radiation to reduce severe dental complications.

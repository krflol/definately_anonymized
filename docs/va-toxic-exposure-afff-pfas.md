# VA Toxic Exposure Note - AFFF/PFAS

This is the public-safe version of the veteran exposure note. It intentionally omits boat, hull, command, exact dates, locations, and names.

## Current Public-Safe Facts

- Former submarine sailor.
- Reported AFFF/PFAS exposure during service.
- Current diagnosis: metastatic squamous cell carcinoma in a patient-right cervical lymph node, p16 block positive.
- Primary site and final stage are pending.

## Practical VA Interpretation

This exposure history changes the VA strategy, but it does not create an automatic PFAS/AFFF presumption.

The strongest public-safe framing is:

1. Protect the date with a VA Intent to File.
2. Work with a VSO or accredited representative.
3. Preserve AFFF/PFAS as a direct service-connection / toxic exposure risk activity theory.
4. Check PACT Act head/neck cancer presumptions separately based on qualifying covered locations or operations.
5. Include radiation, asbestos, solvents, shipyard, fuel/oil, or other submarine occupational exposures only if supported by records or credible statements.
6. Ask whether a TERA medical opinion should address total and combined exposures.

## Evidence Categories

The private claim file should collect:

- service personnel record showing submarine assignment;
- rate/MOS and job duties;
- firefighting or damage-control training and qualifications;
- AFFF storage, handling, maintenance, cleanup, spill, drill, or equipment-contact details;
- frequency, duration, exposure route, and PPE details;
- buddy statements if available;
- toxic exposure screening notes;
- Environmental Health, VET-HOME, or Military Environmental Exposure Assessment notes;
- pathology, staging, and treatment-plan records;
- radiation exposure records only if relevant.

## Exposure Statement Template

Public-safe draft:

> I served in the United States Navy as a submariner and had AFFF/PFAS exposure through shipboard firefighting, damage-control, maintenance, storage, cleanup, drills, spills, contaminated equipment, or related duties. I now have biopsy-confirmed metastatic squamous cell carcinoma in a patient-right cervical lymph node, p16 positive, with primary site and final staging pending. I request review of whether this cancer is service connected based on documented AFFF/PFAS exposure and any other supported submarine occupational exposures, including combined toxic exposure risk activities.

Do not publish the completed version of this statement if it includes boat, hull, command, deployment, exact dates, location, or witness names.

## Questions For VSO / Accredited Representative

- Should the claim be filed now as metastatic squamous cell carcinoma / head-and-neck cancer with primary site pending?
- Should AFFF/PFAS be claimed as a TERA/direct service-connection theory?
- Should the claim request a medical opinion addressing total and combined toxic exposures under 38 U.S.C. 1168?
- Does the service history separately qualify for a PACT Act covered-location/operation presumption?
- Should radiation exposure records be requested?
- Should asbestos, solvents, fuels/oils, shipyard work, or other submarine occupational exposures be included?
- What should be uploaded now versus after PET/CT, oncology plan, and primary-site identification?

## Guardrail

AFFF/PFAS exposure is worth documenting. It should not be overstated as a guaranteed VA presumption. The claim should stay disciplined: current cancer diagnosis, concrete exposure facts, service records, and a medical nexus theory.

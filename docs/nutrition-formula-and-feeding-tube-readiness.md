# Nutrition Formula And Feeding-Tube Readiness

This note is an anonymized planning summary for maintaining oral intake during possible head-and-neck radiation or chemoradiation. It is not medical advice and should be reconciled with oncology nutrition, radiation oncology, and speech/swallow guidance.

## Clinical Reason For This Plan

Radiation oncology guidance in the private record: given current age/function/condition, a prophylactic feeding tube was discussed as optional rather than clearly medically necessary at that moment.

The operational concern is not only medical need. It is also logistics: if oral intake fails during treatment, there may be a lag before an NG tube, PEG/RIG, formula authorization, or other rescue support is actually active.

The goal is to support a reactive-tube strategy safely by:

- maintaining calories/protein orally as long as swallowing is safe;
- keeping a reliable high-calorie liquid nutrition buffer on hand;
- escalating early if intake, hydration, or weight trend fails.

## Product Strategy

Current practical framework:

- **Boost Very High Calorie Creamy Strawberry**: staple / default oral calorie anchor because it is available, calorie dense, and easier to source.
- **TwoCal HN Vanilla**: preferred medical-formula reserve / upgrade when available because it is explicitly positioned for oral or tube feeding and supplemental or sole-source nutrition under medical supervision.

This means Boost can be the reliable floor, while TwoCal can be rotated in or stockpiled as available. The plan should not depend on a product that cannot be reliably obtained.

## Safety And Contaminant Logic

"Zero heavy metals" is not a realistic food claim. The practical risk-reduction approach is:

- prefer mainstream medical-style formulas and oral nutritional supplements over supplement powders;
- prefer strawberry/vanilla/plain over chocolate when otherwise equivalent, because cocoa can add heavy-metal concern;
- avoid "greens", adaptogen, detox, bodybuilding, or broad plant-powder meal replacements as daily near-primary nutrition during treatment;
- avoid stacking extra multivitamins on top of several fortified bottles/day unless oncology nutrition approves;
- avoid using stockpiled formula as a reason to delay hydration, tube, or urgent support if intake becomes unsafe.

Boost VHC Creamy Strawberry is preferred over chocolate in this plan for the contaminant-minimization goal.

## Stockpile Buffer

Working planning assumption: keep enough formula to bridge a realistic outpatient tube-placement/authorization lag.

Practical lag estimate:

- same-week rescue may be possible if symptoms are severe and the team is already engaged;
- outpatient referral/authorization/procedure scheduling can realistically take 1-3 weeks;
- a conservative VA/community-care/mail-order planning buffer is about 30 days.

Case math:

- 1 case = 24 bottles/cartons.
- 4/day uses 1 case in 6 days.
- 5/day uses 1 case in 4.8 days.

Recommended on-hand amount:

| Buffer | Cases | Units | Coverage |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Minimum | 5 | 120 | about 30 days at 4/day |
| Preferred | 6 | 144 | about 36 days at 4/day or 29 days at 5/day |
| Comfortable | 7 | 168 | about 42 days at 4/day or 34 days at 5/day |

Current recommendation:

- Keep **6 cases minimum** once treatment start is near.
- Aim for **7 cases** if storage/cost/rotation are manageable.
- Count Boost VHC and TwoCal HN together. Example: 5 cases Boost plus 2 cases TwoCal equals a 7-case buffer.

Reorder point:

- before treatment: reorder at 4 cases remaining;
- during radiation/chemoradiation: reorder at 5 cases remaining.

## Escalation Rules

This stockpile is a bridge, not a substitute for clinical escalation.

Notify oncology nutrition/radiation promptly if:

- formula need reaches 4/day for more than several days;
- formula need reaches 5/day at all;
- oral intake is under roughly 60% of target for more than a few days;
- hydration is difficult, urine is dark, dizziness occurs, or IV fluids are needed;
- swallowing liquids causes coughing/choking;
- pain, mucositis, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or fatigue prevents formula use;
- weight drops unintentionally.

Ask the care team directly:

> If oral intake drops below target or formula need reaches 4-5 bottles/cartons per day, what is the actual local timeline for NG tube, PEG/RIG referral, IV hydration, formula authorization, and supply delivery?

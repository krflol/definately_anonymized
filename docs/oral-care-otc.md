# OTC Oral Care Notes

This note focuses on over-the-counter products while waiting for dental/radiation-oncology guidance.

## Current Recommendation

Use a gentle fluoride toothpaste as the main toothpaste.

Good OTC profile:

- Fluoride-containing.
- SLS-free if possible.
- Non-whitening.
- Non-charcoal.
- Mild flavor.
- Low irritation.

Reasonable OTC examples:

- Biotene Fluoride Toothpaste Fresh Mint Original.
- Sensodyne Pronamel.

## Hydroxyapatite

Hydroxyapatite, often marketed as nano-hydroxyapatite, is a remineralization ingredient with real dental research behind it. It may be reasonable as an add-on, especially for sensitivity or enamel support.

For this cancer-risk context, do not rely on fluoride-free hydroxyapatite toothpaste as the only cavity-prevention strategy if head-and-neck radiation may be part of treatment. Radiation-related dry mouth can sharply increase tooth-decay risk, and current cancer oral-care guidance still emphasizes fluoride.

Practical approach:

- Fluoride toothpaste at night.
- Hydroxyapatite toothpaste as a second brushing if desired.
- Ask dental/radiation oncology about prescription 5000 ppm fluoride toothpaste and fluoride trays.

## Avoid For Now

- Charcoal toothpaste.
- Whitening or peroxide toothpaste.
- Fluoride-free toothpaste as the only toothpaste.
- Harsh tartar-control formulas if irritating.
- Strong mint if it burns.

## Gum

Sugar-free xylitol gum can stimulate saliva and may help with dry-mouth/cavity risk. Stop if chewing causes jaw pain, mouth sores, or irritation.

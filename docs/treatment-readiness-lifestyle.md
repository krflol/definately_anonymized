# Treatment Readiness And Lifestyle Plan

This plan is supportive. It should not delay staging, oncology visits, biopsy follow-up, or curative-intent treatment planning.

## Goals

- Preserve strength and weight before treatment.
- Reduce inflammation and avoid avoidable treatment complications.
- Protect oral health before possible head-and-neck radiation.
- Build habits that can survive treatment fatigue.
- Track symptoms clearly for clinicians.

## Nutrition

- Prioritize protein at each meal.
- Avoid unintentional weight loss.
- Prefer easy-to-eat, high-calorie, high-protein foods if swallowing becomes uncomfortable.
- Hydrate consistently.
- Ask early for oncology nutrition support.
- See `nutrition-formula-and-feeding-tube-readiness.md` for the current oral formula and stockpile plan.
- Current practical formula strategy: Boost Very High Calorie Creamy Strawberry as the reliable staple / calorie anchor, with TwoCal HN Vanilla as a preferred medical-formula reserve when available.
- Maintain a formula buffer large enough to bridge a realistic outpatient tube-placement / supply-authorization delay.
- Do not let formula stockpiling delay escalation if intake, hydration, or swallowing safety fails.

## Feeding-Tube Readiness

- Radiation oncology discussed prophylactic feeding tube as optional rather than clearly medically necessary at the current functional status.
- A reactive strategy should still have a written escalation plan.
- Ask the team for exact local timelines for NG tube, PEG/RIG, IV hydration, formula authorization, and supply delivery.
- If 4-5 high-calorie bottles/cartons per day become necessary for more than several days, treat that as an escalation signal, not just a stockpile-use signal.

## Exercise

- Maintain daily walking or low-intensity cardio as tolerated.
- Add light resistance training if energy allows.
- Avoid aggressive new training that risks injury or large weight loss.
- Track fatigue and pain.

## Sleep And Recovery

- Keep a consistent sleep window.
- Reduce alcohol and nicotine exposure.
- Treat nicotine cessation as a high-yield intervention if relevant.
- Escalate uncontrolled pain, breathing difficulty, severe swallowing trouble, or rapid growth of the neck mass.

## Oral Health Before Treatment

- Schedule dental evaluation before head-and-neck radiation if radiation is possible.
- Ask about prescription high-fluoride toothpaste or fluoride trays.
- Use a gentle fluoride toothpaste now.
- Avoid whitening/peroxide/charcoal toothpaste and abrasive products.
- Use a soft or extra-soft toothbrush.

## Symptom Tracking

Track:

- Weight.
- Swallowing changes.
- Voice changes.
- Pain.
- Ear pain.
- Bleeding.
- Breathing changes.
- Neck mass size/firmness.
- New lumps.
- Fever/night sweats.
- Fatigue.

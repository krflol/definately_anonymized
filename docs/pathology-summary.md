# Pathology Summary

## Specimen

Patient-right cervical-chain lymph-node conglomerate, needle core biopsy.

## Diagnosis

Metastatic squamous cell carcinoma, moderate to poorly differentiated.

## Immunohistochemistry

- CK5: positive.
- p16: positive with block staining.

## Flow Cytometry

No significant lymphoid immunophenotypic abnormalities were reported.

## Interpretation

The biopsy confirms squamous cell carcinoma in a right cervical lymph node. p16 block positivity raises concern for HPV-mediated carcinoma, commonly from an oropharyngeal primary. The report recommended clinical and radiologic correlation to identify the primary site.

Flow cytometry makes lymphoma less likely as the cause of the biopsied mass.

## Missing Or Pending Details

- Final pathology from the patient-right tonsil removed during diagnostic primary-site workup.
- Final pathology from small patient-right base-of-tongue biopsies.
- Primary tumor site: tonsil, base of tongue, another site, or occult/unsampled.
- If tonsil carcinoma is present: tumor size, margins, lymphovascular invasion, perineural invasion, HPV-specific result, and AJCC Version 9 pathologic T category.
- Written AJCC Version 9 clinical TNM/stage and imaging-detected extranodal-extension status if HPV-associated oropharyngeal SCC is confirmed.
- HPV DNA/RNA or genotype, if ordered.
- PD-L1 CPS, if ordered.
- Tumor-board recommendations.

No neck dissection was performed during the primary-site procedure. The known cervical-node SCC therefore remains established disease, and that operation cannot provide a complete pathologic nodal category or node count.

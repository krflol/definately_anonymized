## Key Clarification

"Metastatic SCC in a cervical lymph node" means cancer is present in a lymph node. It does not automatically mean distant metastatic disease to lung, liver, bone, or other organs.

This distinction matters because HPV-mediated oropharyngeal cancer with neck-node involvement can still be treated with curative intent in many cases.

## Treatment Research Reframe

The better trial question is not "what cutting-edge treatment exists?"

Better:

> Before curative therapy starts, is there a trial for newly diagnosed HPV-associated OPSCC that fits stage, HPV genotype, primary-site status, resectability, and timing without compromising cure?

## Key Cautions

- Perioperative pembrolizumab is real and current, but only fits a defined surgery-based, resectable locally advanced HNSCC lane with PD-L1 CPS criteria.
- De-intensification should not be assumed safe just because HPV-positive disease can have favorable prognosis. Reduced-intensity treatment should be trial-based or specifically tumor-board supported.
- HPV ctDNA / TTMV-HPV DNA may be useful as a baseline and surveillance adjunct, but it does not replace PET/CT, exam, biopsy, or formal staging.
- Supportive care is part of treatment readiness: dental, fluoride, nutrition, speech/swallow, audiology, fertility, and nicotine/alcohol support should be handled early.

## Tight Oncology Question Set

1. What is the exact working diagnosis today: HPV-mediated oropharyngeal SCC, SCC of unknown primary, or another head/neck primary?

3. Has high-risk HPV DNA/RNA testing and genotype been ordered, or only p16?
4. Is PD-L1 CPS being ordered, and would the result change anything in the curative-intent plan?
5. Is treatment expected to be surgery-based, definitive chemoradiation-based, or still undecided?
6. What is the definitive plan for the known cervical-node disease?
7. Does the perioperative pembrolizumab approval apply if a later operation would be the definitive oncologic surgery?
8. Should HPV ctDNA / TTMV-HPV DNA be drawn before definitive nodal treatment, accurately labeled as post-tonsillectomy?
9. Does any newly diagnosed HPV-associated OPSCC trial remain technically available after diagnostic tonsillectomy, without delaying curative care?

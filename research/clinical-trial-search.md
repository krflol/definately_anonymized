# Clinical Trial Search Playbook

## Current Search Frame

Provisional information suggests `M0`, pending confirmation. If confirmed, prioritize newly diagnosed curative-intent HPV-associated oropharyngeal/head-and-neck trials over recurrent/metastatic-only trials.

## Search Terms

- `HPV positive oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma`
- `p16 positive oropharyngeal cancer`
- `HPV16 head and neck squamous cell carcinoma`
- `metastatic squamous neck cancer occult primary`
- `BNT113 pembrolizumab`
- `PRGN-2009 HPV`
- `PDS0101 pembrolizumab HPV16`
- `VB10.16 pembrolizumab`
- `E7 TCR-T HPV`
- `HPV ctDNA NavDx oropharyngeal`
- `deintensification HPV oropharyngeal`

## Eligibility Details To Collect

- Exact diagnosis and stage.
- Basis for `M0`.
- Primary site.
- HPV genotype.
- PD-L1 CPS.
- Prior treatment status.
- Treatment lane: surgery-based, definitive chemoradiation, or undecided.
- Extranodal-extension suspicion.
- Smoking pack-years/risk category.
- HLA type if TCR-T is being considered.
- ECOG performance status.
- Organ function labs.
- Hearing status if cisplatin is possible.
- Autoimmune disease, transplant history, chronic steroids, or immunosuppression.

## Practical Workflow

1. Finish staging first.
2. Ask whether treatment intent is curative.
3. If `M0` and newly diagnosed curative-intent disease are confirmed, ask specifically about newly diagnosed HPV-associated OPSCC trials before standard treatment begins.
4. Ask whether `NCT06223568` or `NCT04900623` are worth screening if HPV-mediated OPSCC is confirmed.
5. If yes, ask for referral immediately because screening can take time.
6. If no, do not delay curative treatment just to keep searching.
7. Repeat the trial search after major decision points: PET/CT, surgery if performed, final pathology, radiation/chemoradiation, and surveillance.

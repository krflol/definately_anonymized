# Clinical Trial Search Playbook

## Current Search Frame

PET/CT supports a provisional local-regional / `cM0` frame. A diagnostic primary-site procedure removed the patient-right tonsil, sampled the patient-right base of tongue, and left the known cervical-node SCC in place. Both new pathology results and the definitive treatment plan remain pending.

Prior diagnostic surgery does not automatically prove that every neoadjuvant trial window is closed. It does change eligibility and specimen availability, so the trial site must review the operative summary, pathology, remaining measurable/biopsy-accessible disease, and planned standard surgery. Trial screening must not delay curative care.

## Search Terms

- `newly diagnosed HPV positive oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma`
- `p16 positive oropharyngeal cancer neoadjuvant`
- `HPV16 head and neck squamous cell carcinoma vaccine`
- `metastatic squamous neck cancer occult primary`
- `PRGN-2009 HPV newly diagnosed`
- `HPV ctDNA NavDx oropharyngeal trial`
- `deintensification HPV oropharyngeal`

## Eligibility Details To Collect

- Tonsil and base-of-tongue pathology.
- Exact diagnosis, AJCC Version 9 stage, and basis for `cM0`.
- Imaging-detected extranodal-extension status.
- HPV-specific result and genotype.
- PD-L1 CPS.
- Exact prior procedure and confirmation that no neck dissection occurred.
- Remaining tumor site that can be biopsied, if a protocol requires it.
- Planned definitive treatment: surgery, radiation/chemoradiation, or undecided.
- Smoking pack-years and quit timing.
- ECOG performance status, organ function, hearing, and neuropathy.
- Autoimmune disease, transplant history, chronic steroids, or immunosuppression.

## Practical Workflow

1. Obtain both new pathology reports and the written AJCC Version 9 stage.
2. Confirm curative intent and the definitive treatment owner.
3. Ask whether a later cancer-removal surgery is actually planned.
4. For `NCT06223568`, send the anonymized operative facts to the NIH site and ask for a criterion-level determination; do not infer eligibility locally.
5. Treat de-intensification studies as a risk tradeoff, not an assumed upgrade over standard therapy.
6. If screening cannot be completed promptly or the team sees no likely benefit, proceed with standard curative treatment.
7. Repeat the search after final pathology, tumor board, definitive treatment, and any recurrence decision point.

## Interpretation Guardrail

`Recruiting` does not mean eligible, locally available, or better than standard care. The required comparison is expected cure, toxicity, logistical delay, and fallback treatment for this exact case.

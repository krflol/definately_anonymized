# Immunotherapy - Checkpoint Inhibitors

## Bottom Line

PD-1 checkpoint inhibitors are established head-and-neck cancer treatments in defined settings. They are relevant to ask about, but they are not automatically the first or only treatment for a newly diagnosed, potentially curable p16-positive neck-node presentation.

If provisional `M0` is confirmed, recurrent/metastatic first-line immunotherapy is less likely to be the immediate lane. The main immunotherapy question becomes whether perioperative pembrolizumab applies if the case is resectable locally advanced HNSCC, PD-L1 CPS >= 1, and surgery-based treatment is planned.

## Why It Matters

Checkpoint inhibitors such as pembrolizumab and nivolumab release immune-system brakes, especially the PD-1/PD-L1 pathway. HPV-positive tumors may be immunologically visible because viral proteins are foreign antigens.

## Current Relevance

- Recurrent/metastatic HNSCC: PD-1 therapy is established in defined settings.
- Resectable locally advanced HNSCC: FDA approved neoadjuvant/adjuvant pembrolizumab in a defined setting for tumors expressing PD-L1 CPS >= 1.
- Clinical trials: PD-1 agents are often combined with HPV vaccines, chemotherapy, radiation, or targeted agents.

## Ask Oncology

- Should PD-L1 CPS be run on the biopsy specimen?
- Does the perioperative pembrolizumab approval apply to this exact stage and treatment plan?
- Is immunotherapy relevant up front, after surgery, with radiation, or only if disease is recurrent/metastatic?
- Are there trials combining PD-1 therapy with HPV-directed therapy before standard treatment?
- If definitive chemoradiation is recommended, is immunotherapy only trial-based or later-line in this exact case?

# Physician Discussion - Emerging Treatment Pro-Con Matrix

> Purpose: bring a current, evidence-grounded line of questioning to physicians and tumor board. This is not a request to bypass standard staging or curative-intent care.

## Current Case Anchor

- Known diagnosis: metastatic squamous cell carcinoma in a patient-right cervical lymph node.
- Biomarkers so far: CK5 positive; p16 block positive.
- Most likely frame until proven otherwise: HPV-mediated oropharyngeal SCC presenting as a neck node, or metastatic squamous neck cancer with occult primary.
- PET/CT supports a provisional local-regional / `cM0` frame.
- Diagnostic primary-site workup removed the patient-right tonsil and sampled the patient-right base of tongue; both pathology results are pending. No neck dissection occurred, so known nodal SCC remains.
- Missing gating data: new pathology, primary site, AJCC Version 9 stage, imaging-detected extranodal-extension status, HPV DNA/RNA or genotype, PD-L1 CPS, resectability, smoking risk category, performance status, cisplatin eligibility, and definitive treatment plan.

Outside-review synthesis: the first task is to determine diagnosis and treatment lane, not to find more experimental options. Ask whether this is HPV-mediated oropharyngeal SCC, SCC of unknown primary, or another head/neck primary; then confirm the new pathology, cT/cN/cM, AJCC Version 9 stage, node details, HPV-specific testing/genotype, PD-L1 CPS, and whether treatment is surgery-based or definitive chemoradiation-based.

M0 update: if `M0` is confirmed, prioritize curative-intent locoregional pathways and newly diagnosed trials. Recurrent/metastatic systemic therapy becomes less likely as the immediate lane unless later imaging changes the disease setting.

## How To Frame This With A Physician

Use this sentence if an option is brushed off too quickly:

> I understand this may not apply to my exact stage. Can we document which criterion makes it not applicable: primary site, stage, resectability, PD-L1 CPS, HPV genotype, trial eligibility, toxicity, or timing?

The goal is not to demand every new therapy. The goal is to prevent a current approved or trial-supported option from being dismissed as "fringe" without checking whether the patient-specific criteria fit.

## Perioperative Pembrolizumab If Surgery-Based Locally Advanced HNSCC

Case for:

- This is not fringe if the exact criteria fit. FDA approved pembrolizumab for adults with resectable locally advanced HNSCC whose tumors express PD-L1 CPS >= 1, as neoadjuvant treatment before surgery, then adjuvant treatment with radiotherapy with or without cisplatin, then pembrolizumab alone.
- The evidence base is KEYNOTE-689. The FDA summary reported improved event-free survival, with median EFS 59.7 months in the pembrolizumab arm versus 29.6 months in the control arm.
- It is directly relevant to a newly diagnosed, curative-intent setting if surgery is planned.

Case against:

- It only applies if the case is resectable locally advanced HNSCC and PD-L1 CPS >= 1.
- It may not apply if treatment is definitive chemoradiation without surgery, if primary site/stage do not match the label, or if PD-L1 CPS is negative/unknown.
- Immunotherapy can cause immune-related adverse events and should not delay standard curative-intent planning.
- The approved sequence starts before definitive surgery. The diagnostic tonsillectomy does not by itself settle whether a later definitive-surgery sequence remains possible, and postoperative pembrolizumab alone is not the same regimen.

Questions:

- Is this resectable locally advanced HNSCC?
- Is surgery likely?
- Can PD-L1 CPS be run now?
- If PD-L1 CPS is >= 1 and surgery is planned, does the FDA-approved perioperative pembrolizumab approach apply?
- If not, which exact criterion rules it out?

## PD-1 Therapy For Recurrent/Metastatic Or Unresectable HNSCC

Case for:

- Pembrolizumab and nivolumab are established FDA-approved therapies in defined recurrent/metastatic HNSCC settings.
- If staging shows distant disease, or if disease is unresectable/recurrent, these are mainstream systemic therapy questions.

Case against:

- If this is a first presentation with regional nodal disease and no distant metastasis, curative-intent local/regional treatment may be the priority.
- PD-L1 CPS, performance status, autoimmune history, organ function, and disease burden matter.

Questions:

- If staging shows distant metastatic disease, what first-line systemic regimen would you recommend?
- What is the PD-L1 CPS?
- Would pembrolizumab monotherapy or pembrolizumab plus chemotherapy be considered if metastatic/unresectable disease is found?
- If disease is not distant metastatic, why is recurrent/metastatic PD-1 therapy not the correct category right now?

## Therapeutic HPV Vaccines And mRNA Vaccines

Case for:

- This is the most biologically aligned emerging category if the tumor is HPV-mediated, especially HPV16.
- HPV-driven cancers express viral antigens such as E6 and E7.
- Trials include BNT113 plus pembrolizumab, VB10.16 plus pembrolizumab, PRGN-2009 strategies including a newly diagnosed HPV-associated oropharyngeal/head-and-neck cancer study, PDS0101 plus pembrolizumab, and HB-200 approaches.

Case against:

- These are not standard approved therapies for newly diagnosed p16-positive neck-node disease.
- Many trials require HPV16 specifically, not just p16 positivity.
- Many trials are for recurrent/metastatic or unresectable disease.
- Trial participation should not delay a known curative-intent plan unless tumor board agrees.

Questions:

- Can the tumor be tested for HPV DNA/RNA and genotype, especially HPV16?
- Are any therapeutic HPV vaccine trials appropriate before standard treatment begins?
- If none apply now, should the trial search repeat after staging or after definitive treatment?

## HPV-Targeted TCR-T / Engineered Cell Therapy

Case for:

- HPV16 E6/E7 are strong theoretical targets for engineered T-cell receptor therapy.
- A recruiting phase 2 E7 TCR-T trial includes HPV-associated cancers and HPV-positive oropharyngeal SCC.

Case against:

- This is not standard first-line therapy for a newly diagnosed potentially curable presentation.
- TCR-T commonly requires a specific HLA type, specialized center access, lymphodepletion, and strict eligibility.

Questions:

- Would HLA typing be useful now, or only if trial screening becomes relevant?
- If HPV16 is confirmed, are HPV16 E6/E7 TCR-T trials appropriate only after recurrence/metastasis?
- If this becomes relevant later, which center would you refer to?

## HPV ctDNA / TTMV-HPV DNA Monitoring

Case for:

- This is one of the most practical emerging tools to ask about early because a pretreatment baseline may be useful.
- HPV ctDNA/TTMV-HPV DNA can potentially support surveillance after treatment and may help detect molecular recurrence.
- Multiple clinical trials are using HPV ctDNA to guide risk-adapted or de-intensified therapy.

Case against:

- It is not a substitute for PET/CT, endoscopy, physical exam, biopsy, or formal staging.
- A positive result needs an action plan; a negative result does not guarantee absence of disease.
- ctDNA-guided treatment changes should generally be trial-based or tumor-board supported.

Questions:

- Should HPV ctDNA be drawn before definitive nodal treatment, with the timing labeled as post-tonsillectomy?
- Which assay would you use, and is it available through my system?
- Would the result change treatment now, or mainly help with surveillance?
- Are there ctDNA-guided trials appropriate for the final stage?

## De-Intensification / Risk-Adapted HPV-Positive Treatment

Case for:

- HPV-mediated oropharyngeal SCC often has better prognosis than HPV-negative disease.
- Long-term toxicity matters: swallowing, saliva, dental health, hearing, taste, fibrosis, and feeding-tube risk.
- Risk-adapted approaches are actively studied.

Case against:

- De-intensification can be dangerous if misapplied.
- RTOG 1016 and De-ESCALaTE HPV showed that replacing cisplatin with cetuximab worsened cancer outcomes in HPV-positive oropharyngeal cancer.
- A major de-intensification trial, NRG-HN005, did not establish its reduced-intensity arms as noninferior to standard chemoradiation.
- Outside a protocol or specific tumor-board recommendation, reducing intensity can trade less toxicity for higher recurrence risk.

Questions:

- Is this low-risk, intermediate-risk, or high-risk after final staging?
- What factors drive that risk category?
- Is any de-intensified approach standard for this exact situation, or only trial-based?
- If cetuximab is discussed, is it because of cisplatin ineligibility or as de-intensification?

## EGFR-Directed Therapy / Cetuximab And Newer Targeted Agents

Case for:

- Cetuximab is an established head-and-neck cancer drug in selected settings.
- It may be relevant if cisplatin is contraindicated or in recurrent/metastatic planning.

Case against:

- In HPV-positive oropharyngeal cancer, cetuximab should not be assumed to be a gentler equivalent replacement for cisplatin.
- Newer targeted agents are generally trial-stage and more relevant to recurrent/metastatic disease.

Questions:

- Am I cisplatin eligible? If not, why not?
- If cisplatin is not recommended, what alternative gives the best cancer control?
- Is cetuximab being proposed because of a medical contraindication or because of de-intensification?

## Direct CRISPR / In Vivo HPV Gene Editing

Case for:

- The scientific rationale is coherent: HPV-driven cancers depend on viral oncogenes such as E6 and E7.
- Gene-editing medicine is a real field.

Case against:

- For HPV-positive head-and-neck SCC, direct CRISPR editing of the tumor is not a current standard or realistic near-term treatment option.
- Most work is preclinical or very early translational.
- This is the category most likely to be fairly called fringe if presented as a treatment option outside a legitimate clinical trial.

Questions:

- Are there any legitimate registered clinical trials of direct HPV gene editing for this disease?
- If not, should CRISPR be treated as a monitor-only research topic?
- Are engineered T-cell trials more realistic than direct tumor gene editing?

## Personalized Neoantigen / Individualized mRNA Vaccines

Case for:

- Personalized cancer vaccines are a real and active field, especially in combination with checkpoint inhibitors.
- Tumor sequencing may reveal trial options.

Case against:

- For HPV-mediated disease, shared viral antigens such as E6/E7 are often more direct targets than private mutation neoantigens.
- Personalized vaccine manufacturing takes time and is usually trial-based.

Questions:

- Is tumor sequencing planned or useful in the current setting?
- Are personalized vaccine trials relevant, or are HPV antigen vaccine trials more relevant?
- Would a personalized vaccine trial require recurrent/metastatic disease?

## Highest-Value Questions For The Next Oncology Visit

1. Is `M0` confirmed, and what imaging or assessment is it based on?
2. What is the exact working diagnosis today: HPV-mediated oropharyngeal SCC, SCC of unknown primary, or another head/neck primary?
3. Which new specimen identifies the primary, and what are cT, cN, cM, AJCC Version 9 stage, node size, laterality, and imaging-detected extranodal-extension status?
4. Has high-risk HPV DNA/RNA testing and genotype been ordered, or only p16?
5. Is PD-L1 CPS being ordered, and would the result change anything in the curative-intent plan?
6. Is treatment expected to be surgery-based, definitive chemoradiation-based, or still undecided?
7. If another definitive surgery is planned and PD-L1 CPS >= 1, does the perioperative pembrolizumab approval apply to this exact case?
8. If definitive chemoradiation is likely, what is the standard regimen and why?
9. Is cisplatin likely, and is the patient eligible for it?
10. Should HPV ctDNA / TTMV-HPV DNA be drawn before definitive nodal treatment, accurately labeled as post-tonsillectomy?
11. Does `NCT06223568` or another newly diagnosed trial remain technically screenable after diagnostic tonsillectomy, without delaying curative care?
12. Will the case go to head-and-neck tumor board?
13. If an option is not appropriate, which exact criterion rules it out?
